Mentions of raclette cheese date as far back as the 12th century when shepherds and farmers at that time would place the cheese next to a campfire to melt it and then spread it on other foods for a filling meal. As it was easy to transport up to the mountains, this dish become popular among people living in the Alps valleys, and over time it ascended to a national delicacy.

Raclette cheese is made from high-quality cow milk and then pressed into a wheel shape before getting soaked in a salt brine. The cheese is aged from three to six months, and depending on the cheese-making region, it will have a slightly different aroma and flavour. Regular accompaniments are potatoes, pickled vegetables (like cornichons or olives) and cured meats.
